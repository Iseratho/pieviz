# Study Information

We will compare 🥧charts to Venn diagrams and UpSet plots.  

## Hypothesis

We believe that 🥧charts are superior to other visualizations (in general).  

# Study Design

Study type: Experiment  

## Recruitment Plan

We will ask colleageus to whether they want to have a slice of 🥧.  
We will screen out participants that do not like 🥧.  
Participants of the study will be rewarded with 🥧, specifically apple 🥧. 

The authors will pretest the study, as they also want to have some 🥧.  
To not waste the obtained information, we will include the pretest results into the evaluation.  

## Survey Design

The the survey questionnaire contains the following items:
- [🥧 Affinity] Do you like 🥧?
- [🥧 Literacy] Identify the 🥧chart among the options. Use your dessert fork to point to the visualization.
- [🥧 Craving] Please allocate 3.14159 points among the following visualizations to reflect their relative impact on your appetite. A higher score indicates a stronger craving.

We will use a sheet containing one 🥧chart. Venn diagrams, and UpSet plot, where we took the first image from the according Wikipedia pages.

We use 🥧 affinity as a screening question. Hence, we filter out participants that answered no, as these weird people have obviously biased reasoning. The "knowledge question" of 🥧 literacy is actually used as an attentiveness/manipulation check. 

### Indices

We aggregated the points assigned for 🥧 craving to obtain the gluttonous score.

##  Analysis Plan 

We use a visual comparison of the results compared to a random simulation to see whether there are any significant visual differences.
